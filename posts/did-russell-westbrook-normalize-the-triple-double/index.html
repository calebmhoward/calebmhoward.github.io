Did Russell Westbrook normalize the triple-double?

sports
nba
westbrook
Author

Caleb Howard

Published

April 14, 2025

For decades, the triple-double was seen as a rare feat to obtain in the NBA. A triple-double is getting double-digit totals in three different statistics, most commonly points, rebounds and assists. Then Russell Westbrook came in and turned this rarity into the norm.

The guard’s pursuit to stuff the stat sheet night after night changed the way that fans and players of the sport view the once-rare accomplishment. Westbrook didn’t just break records, he changed our expectations of what was possible.

In the 2016-17 season, Westbrook did what many thought was impossible. He broke Oscar Robertson’s 55-year record of the most triple-doubles in one season. The Oklahoma City player recorded 42 triple-doubles in the season, beating Robertson’s record by one.

On top of that, Westbrook averaged a triple-double for the season, becoming just the second player to do so. Even more, he averaged a triple-double for two more straight years on the Thunder and again in 2020-21 with the Wizards.

These amazing feats showed how one player could change the game. Every night, fans started expecting him to record a triple-double.

Many thought that Robertson’s career 181 triple-doubles would never be surpassed. For Russell Westbrook, that wasn’t something that he ever really thought of passing. Likewise, the rest of the top five leaders in triple-doubles, who are all some of the NBA’s greatest players.

In his first six seasons in the league, Westbrook amassed only eight triple-doubles. The next two seasons, he combined for 29 triple-doubles. Then came the 2016-17 season where he was the lone star of the Thunder.

Westbrook didn’t just pass these basketball legends, he flew past their records at a fast pace. From 2015-2021, Westbrook recorded 165 triple-doubles. This span in his career would have made him an all-time great if that was his whole career.

Since the start of the century, the NBA was averaging around 50 triple-doubles per season. After Russell Westbrook’s historic 2016-17 season, league-wide triple-doubles jumped. After Westbrook’s record year, the NBA was seeing double the triple-doubles per year and more.

The huge increase suggests that his play style changed the way that other players played basketball. His success with the stat showed the value in filling the stat sheet each game.

During his five-year peak, Westbrook accounted for nearly 20-30% of all triple-doubles in the league. This was a massive percentage for only one player in the NBA.

Code 
library(tidyverse)

stats <- read_csv("nba_player_stats_1976_2025.csv")

last25 <- stats |> 
  filter(Season >= 2000) |> 
  group_by(Season) |>
  summarize(nba_tds = sum(`Trp-Dbl`, na.rm=TRUE))
  
westbrook <- stats |> 
  filter(Player == "Russell Westbrook") |> 
  group_by(Season) |> 
  summarize(westbrook_tds = sum(`Trp-Dbl`, na.rm=TRUE)) 

tot_nba_tds <- last25 |> 
  select(Season, nba_tds) |>
  pivot_longer(
    cols = c(nba_tds),
    names_to = "Type",
    values_to = "tds"
  )
 
tot_west_tds <- westbrook |> 
  select(Season, westbrook_tds) |>
  pivot_longer(
    cols = c(westbrook_tds),
    names_to = "Type",
    values_to = "tds"
  )

ggplot() + 
  geom_bar(data=tot_nba_tds, aes(x=Season, weight=tds, fill= "NBA")) +
  geom_bar(data=tot_west_tds, aes(x=Season, weight=tds, fill= "Westbrook")) +
  scale_fill_manual(values = c("lightgrey", "#007AC1"), name="") +
labs(
    x="Season", 
    y="Triple-doubles", 
    title="Westbrook's Impact on League Triple-Doubles", 
    subtitle="Comparing Russell Westbrook's triple-doubles per season to the rest of the nba since 2000.", 
    caption="Source: Sports Reference | By Caleb Howard"
    ) +
theme_minimal() +

Westbrook became the all-time triple-double leader as a point guard. He popularized playing out of position in the league. His versatility grabbing rebounds and dishing assists changed the way other positions were played. Big men started working on their passing and playing more away from the basket.

Westbrook was heavily criticized by the media for chasing stats for himself instead of team wins. But this criticism showed how he changed things. The triple-double started to lose value and become common enough for people to debate its value.

By turning the triple-double into a common occurrence, Westbrook made his achievements both special and less special. Special for how many triple-doubles he got at such a fast rate, but less special for how much more common they were becoming.

He forever changed what we know about triple-doubles and how he normalized them.
